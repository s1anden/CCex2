Module 4: LeChatlier’s Principle: Response to Stress


In this walkthrough we further discuss how LeChatelier's principle applies when the "stress" to a system at equilibrium is a change in concentration of products or reactants or the volume or temperature of the system.

    Summary: Any kind of stress to the system will cause the system to shift back to equilibrium.

    • A forward shift results in more products being made, while a reverse shift results in more reactants.
    • Changing the amounts of products and reactants will cause a shift:
      • Adding reactants causes a forward shift; adding products causes a reverse shift.
    • Decreasing the volume increases pressure and causes a forward shift (recall that the forward reaction results in fewer total gas molecules).
      • Conversely, increasing the volume decreases pressure and the reaction will shift be in the reverse direction.
    • Changes in temperature also cause reactions to shift.
      • If a reaction is exothermic (heat is a product), adding heat will cause a reverse shift, and vice versa.
      • If a reaction is endothermic (heat is a reactant), adding heat will cause a forward shift, and vice versa.
    • Neither changing concentration nor volume affect the equilibrium constant, K. However, changing the temperature does affect K because heat changes the rate constants of the reaction. Module 5 will explain how the equilibrium constant is related to reaction rates and rate constants.
