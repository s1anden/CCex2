Module 7: Manipulating K and chemical reactions


In this module, we will consider how Q and K change when we manipulate a chemical reaction by doubling it, reversing it, or combining it with another reaction.

    Summary: Doubling a reaction doubles the free energy, and squares K. Reversing a reaction changes the sign of ΔG, and K will be the inverse of the forward reaction. When reactions are combined, Hess' Law tells us that ΔG will be additive, and the equilibrium constant will be the product of the individual Ks.
